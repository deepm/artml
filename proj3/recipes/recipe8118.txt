Recipe By:Bron "I make this cake frequently; it's a standard recipe that makes the rounds at church regularly--at home in Texas. Now, I'm in Pennsylvania and am introducing it to my northern friends and fellow church-goers:). Enjoy it again! EXCELLENT with vanilla bean ice cream." Ingredients 1/4 cup and 1 teaspoon butter 1/4 cup and 1 teaspoon shortening 1 (1 ounce) squares unsweetened chocolate 1/2 cup and 1 tablespoon water 1 cup and 2 tablespoons unbleached all-purpose flour 1 cup and 2 tablespoons white sugar 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon 1/2 teaspoon baking soda 1/4 cup and 1 teaspoon buttermilk 1 eggs, beaten 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract 1/4 cup and 1 teaspoon butter 1 (1 ounce) squares unsweetened chocolate, melted 3 tablespoons and 1 teaspoon milk 9 ounces confectioners' sugar 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract 1/2 cup and 1 tablespoon chopped pecans Directions Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Grease one 9x13 inch cake pan. In a small saucepan, combine 1/2 cup butter, 1/2 cup shortening, 2 ounces chocolate, and water. Heat until chocolate is melted; set aside. In a large bowl, combine flour, sugar, cinnamon, and baking soda. Make a well in the center, and pour in buttermilk, eggs, and vanilla. Add melted butter/chocolate mixture, and beat until smooth. Pour batter into prepared pan. Bake in preheated oven for 20 to 25 minutes, or until cake tests done. Five minutes before cake is done, make frosting. To make frosting: In a saucepan, combine 1/2 cup butter, 2 ounces chocolate, and 6 tablespoons milk. Heat until bubbles form around the edge; remove from heat. Beat in confectioners' sugar, 1 teaspoon vanilla, and nuts. Frost cake while still warm. Allow to cool totally before cutting.
