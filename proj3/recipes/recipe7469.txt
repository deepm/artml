Recipe By:Barb Oke "Delicious served warm with ice cream. If you don't have buttermilk, you may substitute sour milk. Stir 1 tablespoon of vinegar or lemon juice together with 1 cup of milk and let stand for 10 minutes." Ingredients 1/4 cup and 1 teaspoon butter 3/4 cup and 1 tablespoon and 1 teaspoon white sugar 1/2 egg 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract 1 cup and 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour 1/2 teaspoon baking soda 1/8 teaspoon salt 1/2 cup and 1 tablespoon buttermilk 1 cup and 2 tablespoons rhubarb, chopped 1-3/4 teaspoons all-purpose flour 2 tablespoons and 3/4 teaspoon butter 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon 1/2 cup and 1 tablespoon packed brown sugar Directions In a large bowl, cream together butter or margarine and sugar. Beat in egg and vanilla. In another bowl, sift together 2 cups flour, soda, and salt. Add sifted ingredients alternately with buttermilk to creamed mixture. Toss rhubarb with 1 tablespoon flour, and stir into batter. Spoon batter into buttered 9 x 13 inch pan, and smooth the surface. Blend together 1/4 cup butter or margarine, cinnamon, and brown sugar; sprinkle evenly over batter. Bake at 350 degrees F (175 degrees C) for 45 minutes.
