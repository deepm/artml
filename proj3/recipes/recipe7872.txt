Recipe By:Rory "This is an old recipe from my aunt. It is both famous and infamous in our family. It is absolutely irresistible." Ingredients 3/4 cup and 1 tablespoon and 1 teaspoon buttermilk 3/4 teaspoon baking soda 1/3 cup and 1 tablespoon and 1 teaspoon butter 1/3 cup and 1 tablespoon and 1 teaspoon shortening 1-2/3 cups white sugar 4-1/4 eggs 3/4 teaspoon vanilla extract 3/4 cup and 1 tablespoon and 1 teaspoon flaked coconut 3/4 teaspoon baking powder 1-2/3 cups all-purpose flour 7 ounces cream cheese 1/3 cup and 1 tablespoon and 1 teaspoon butter 3/4 teaspoon vanilla extract 3-1/3 cups confectioners' sugar 1 tablespoon and 2 teaspoons light cream 1/3 cup and 1 tablespoon and 1 teaspoon chopped walnuts 3/4 cup and 1 tablespoon and 1 teaspoon sweetened flaked coconut Directions Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Grease three 9 inch round cake pans. In a small bowl, dissolve the baking soda in the buttermilk; set aside. In a large bowl, cream together 1/2 cup butter, shortening and white sugar until light and fluffy. Mix in the eggs, buttermilk mixture, 1 teaspoon vanilla, 1 cup coconut, baking powder and flour. Stir until just combined. Pour batter into the prepared pans. Bake in the preheated oven for 30 to 35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center of the cake comes out clean. Allow to cool. To Make Frosting: In a medium bowl, combine cream cheese, 1/2 cup butter, 1 teaspoon vanilla and confectioners' sugar. Beat until light and fluffy. Mix in a small amount of cream to attain the desired consistency. Stir in chopped nuts and remaining flaked coconut. Spread between layers and on top and sides of cooled cake.
