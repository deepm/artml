Recipe By:Carol "The fruitcake from this French recipe uses candied orange peel, walnuts, and raisins to make a fruitcake requiring no aging." Ingredients 1/2 cup and 2 tablespoons candied orange peel 1/2 cup and 1 tablespoon and 2 teaspoons coarsely chopped walnuts 1/3 cup and 1 tablespoon and 1 teaspoon raisins 1/3 cup and 1 tablespoon and 1 teaspoon golden raisins 1 tablespoon and 2 teaspoons all-purpose flour 1/3 cup and 1 tablespoon and 1 teaspoon butter, softened 1/3 cup and 1 tablespoon and 1 teaspoon white sugar 2 tablespoons and 1/4 teaspoon honey 1-3/4 eggs 1 tablespoon and 3/4 teaspoon light cream 1 tablespoon and 2 teaspoons dark rum 3/4 teaspoon vanilla extract 1-1/4 cups all-purpose flour 1/2 teaspoon baking powder Directions Toss the candied orange peel, walnuts, and raisins with 2 tablespoons flour. Set aside. In a large bowl, cream the butter with the sugar and honey. Beat in the egg, then the cream or milk, rum, and vanilla. Stir together the remaining 1 1/2 cups flour and the baking powder; beat into the creamed mixture. Stir in the fruits and nuts. Turn the batter into a greased and floured 9 x 5 inch loaf pan. Bake in a preheated 350 degrees F (175 degrees C) oven for 10 minutes. Lower the heat to 325 degrees F (165 degrees C). Bake the cake 45 minutes longer, or until it tests done with a toothpick. Transfer to a rack to cool.
