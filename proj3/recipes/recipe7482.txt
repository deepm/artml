Recipe By:Allison Hancock "A lady from our church makes this every year for her daughter." Ingredients 3/4 cup and 1 tablespoon and 1 teaspoon semisweet chocolate chips 1/4 cup and 1 teaspoon butter, melted 1/3 cup and 1 tablespoon and 1 teaspoon chopped pecans 1-2/3 cups all-purpose flour 3/4 teaspoon baking soda 3/4 teaspoon salt 1/3 cup and 1 tablespoon and 1 teaspoon butter 1-1/4 cups white sugar 1-3/4 eggs 3/4 teaspoon vanilla extract 1 cup and 1 tablespoon buttermilk 3/4 cup and 1 tablespoon and 1 teaspoon heavy whipping cream 1 tablespoon and 2 teaspoons white sugar 3/4 cup and 1 tablespoon and 1 teaspoon flaked coconut Directions Grease and flour bottom of two 9 inch round cake pans. Preheat oven to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C). Melt 1/3 cup chocolate morsels. Combine melted butter or margarine with coconut, pecans, and 2/3 cup chocolate morsels; set aside. Cream butter or margarine in a large mixing bowl. Gradually add 1 1/2 cups sugar, and beat until fluffy. Add eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition. Blend in melted chocolate and vanilla extract. Combine the flour, baking soda, and salt. Add these dry ingredients alternately with buttermilk to the creamed mixture, beginning and ending with dry ingredients. Mix well after each addition. Pour batter into greased pans. Sprinkle with coconut mixture. Bake for 30 to 40 minutes, or until cake springs back when touched lightly in center. Cool cake on wire racks. Beat cream with 2 tablespoons of sugar until stiff.
